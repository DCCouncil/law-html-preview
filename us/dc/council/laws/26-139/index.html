D.C. Law 26-139. Body-Worn Camera Transparency for Use of Force Temporary Amendment Act of 2026.

AN ACT

To amend, on a temporary basis, section 3004 of the Body-Worn Camera Regulation and Reporting Requirements Act of 2015 and Chapter 39 of Title 24 of the District of Columbia Municipal Regulations to require the release of body-worn camera recordings of Metropolitan Police Department Officers present in the event of an officer-involved death or serious use of force by a law enforcement officer other than a Metropolitan Police Department officer.

BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this act may be cited as the "Body-Worn Camera Transparency for Use of Force Temporary Amendment Act of 2026".

Sec. 2. Section 3004 of the Body-Worn Camera Regulation and Reporting Requirements Act of 2015, effective October 22, 2015 (D.C. Law 21-36; D.C. Official Code § 5-116.33), is amended as follows:

(a) Subsection (c)(2)(A) is amended as follows:

(1) Sub-subparagraph (i) is amended as follows:

(A) Sub-sub-subparagraph (I) is amended to read as follows:

"(I) The names and body-worn camera recordings of all Metropolitan Police Department officers, as well as the names, as available, of all other law enforcement officers, directly involved in the officer-involved death or serious use of force; and".

(B) Sub-sub-subparagraph (II) is amended by striking the phrase "; and" and inserting a semicolon in its place.

(2) A new sub-subparagraph (i-I) is added to read as follows:

"(i-I) Notwithstanding sub-subparagraph (i) of this subparagraph, for incidents occurring between August 1, 2025, and the effective date of the Body-Worn Camera Transparency for Use of Force Emergency Amendment Act of 2026, effective March 23, 2026 (D.C. Act 26-282; 73 DCR 5081) ("Body-Worn Camera Transparency Emergency Act"), within 10 business days after the effective date of the Body-Worn Camera Transpsarency Emergency Act, publicly release:

"(I) The names and body-worn camera recordings of all Metropolitan Police Department officers, as well as the names, as available, of all other law enforcement officers, directly involved in the officer-involved death or serious use of force; and

"(II) A description of the incident; and".

(3) Sub-subparagraph (ii) is amended by striking the phrase "; and" and inserting the phrase "; provided, that for all officer-involved deaths since August 1, 2025, the information reported pursuant to this sub-subparagraph shall include the names of all Metropolitan Police Department officers as well as other law enforcement officers directly involved in the officer-involved death; and" in its place.

(b) Subsection (e)(1) is amended to read as follows:

"(e)(1) For any incident involving an officer-involved death directly involving an MPD officer or serious use of force directly involving an MPD officer, MPD officers shall not review any body-worn camera recordings to assist in initial report writing; provided, that MPD officers may review body-worn camera recordings to assist in initial report writing for incidents involving an officer-involved death directly involving a law enforcement officer other than an MPD officer or serious use of force directly involving a law enforcement officer other than an MPD officer prior to the effective date of the Body-Worn Camera Transpsarency Emergency Act.".

(c) Subsection (g) is amended as follows:

(1) New paragraphs (1A) and (1B) are added to read as follows:

"(1A) "Law enforcement officer" means any officer, agent, or employee of the United States or any state or local government authorized by law or by a government agency to engage in or supervise the prevention, detection, or investigation of any violation of criminal or civil law.

"(1B) "MPD" means the Metropolitan Police Department.".

(2) Paragraph (3) is amended as follows:

(A) Subparagraph (A) is amended by striking the phrase "Metropolitan Police Department officer" and inserting the phrase "MPD officer, or any other law enforcement officer while an MPD officer is present" in its place.

(B) Subparagraph (B) is amended by striking the phrase "Metropolitan Police Department officer" and inserting the phrase "MPD officer, or any other law enforcement officer while an MPD officer is present" in its place.

(C) The lead-in language of subparagraph (C) is amended by striking the phrase "Metropolitan Police Department officer" and inserting the phrase "MPD officer, or any other law enforcement officer while an MPD officer is present" in its place.

(D) Subparagraph (D) is amended by striking the phrase "Metropolitan Police Department canine" and inserting the phrase "MPD canine, or canine of any other law enforcement officer while an MPD officer is present" in its place.

Sec. 3. Chapter 39 of Title 24 of the District of Columbia Municipal Regulations is amended as follows:

(a) Section 3900 is amended as follows:

(1) Subsection 3900.9 is amended to read as follows:

"3900.9.(a) For any incident involving an officer-involved death directly involving an MPD member or a serious use of force directly involving an MPD member, MPD members shall not review any BWC recordings to assist in initial report writing; provided, that MPD members may review body-worn camera recordings to assist in initial report writing for incidents involving an officer-involved death directly involving a law enforcement officer other than an MPD member or serious use of force directly involving a law enforcement officer other than an MPD member prior to the effective date of the Body-Worn Camera Transparency for Use of Force Emergency Amendment Act of 2026, effective March 23, 2026 (D.C. Act 26-282; 73 DCR 5081).

"(b) For an incident other than those described in paragraph (a) of this subsection, members shall indicate, when writing any initial or subsequent reports, whether the member viewed BWC footage prior to writing the report and specify what BWC footage the member viewed.".

(2) Subsection 3900.10(a)(1) is amended as follows:

(A) Sub-subparagraph (A) is amended as follows:

(i) Sub-sub-subparagraph (i) is amended to read as follows:

"(i) The names and body-worn camera recordings of all Metropolitan Police Department officers, as well as the names, as available, of all other law enforcement officers, directly involved in the officer-involved death or serious use of force; and".

(ii) Sub-sub-subparagraph (ii) is amended by striking the phrase "; and" and inserting a semicolon in its place.

(B) A new sub-subparagraph (A-i) is added to read as follows:

"(A-i) Notwithstanding sub-subparagraph (A) of this subparagraph, for incidents occurring between August 1, 2025, and the effective date of the Body-Worn Camera Transparency for Use of Force Emergency Amendment Act of 2026, effective March 23, 2026 (D.C. Act 26-282; 73 DCR 5081), within 10 business days after the effective date of the Body-Worn Camera Transparency for Use of Force Emergency Amendment Act of 2026, effective March 23, 2026 (D.C. Act 26-282; 73 DCR 5081), publicly release:

"(i) The names and body-worn camera recordings of all Metropolitan Police Department officers, as well as the names, as available, of all other law enforcement officers, directly involved in the officer-involved death or serious use of force; and

"(ii) A description of the incident; and".

(C) Sub-subparagraph (B) is amended by striking the phrase "; and" and inserting the phrase "; provided, that for all officer-involved deaths since August 1, 2025, the information reported pursuant to this sub-subparagraph shall include the names of all Metropolitan Police Department officers as well as other law enforcement officers directly involved in the officer-involved death; and" in its place.

(b) Section 3999.1 is amended by adding a new definition between the definitions of "FOIA" and "Metadata" to read as follows:

" "Law enforcement officer" - means any officer, agent, or employee of the United States or any state or local government authorized by law or by a government agency to engage in or supervise the prevention, detection, or investigation of any violation of criminal or civil law.".

Sec. 4. Applicability.

This act shall apply as of August 1, 2025.

Sec. 5. Fiscal impact statement.

The Council adopts the fiscal impact statement of the Budget Director as the fiscal impact statement required by section 4a of the General Legislative Procedures Act of 1975, approved October 16, 2006 (120 Stat. 2038; D.C. Official Code § 1-301.47a).

Sec. 6. Effective date.

(a) This act shall take effect following approval by the Mayor (or in the event of veto by the Mayor, action by the Council to override the veto) and a 30-day period of congressional review as provided in section 602(c)(1) of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act, approved December 24, 1973 (87 Stat. 813; D.C. Official Code § 1-206.02(c)(1)).

(b) This act shall expire after 225 days of its having taken effect.

Legislative History (LIMS)

Law 26-139, the “Body-Worn Camera Transparency for Use of Force Temporary Amendment Act of 2026,” was introduced in the Council and assigned Bill No. 26-617 which was retained by the Council. The bill was adopted on first and second readings on Mar. 3, 2026, and Mar. 31, 2026, respectively. After mayoral review, it was assigned Act No. 26-305 on Apr. 22, 2026, and transmitted to Congress for its review. D.C. Law 26-139 became effective June 11, 2026.
